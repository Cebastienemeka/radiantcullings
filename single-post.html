A Practical AI Adoption Roadmap for Small and Medium Businesses

10 July 2026 Radiant Cullings Blogs Team 9 min read

Many small and medium businesses begin by experimenting with tools because they are popular. A more effective approach is to start with a recurring business challenge, understand the people and information involved, then test whether AI can improve the work safely and economically.

AI is not a single product. It includes writing assistants, customer-service tools, document analysis, forecasting, content generation, workflow automation, data classification, and many other applications. The right use case depends on the organisation, its data, its processes, its risks, and the capability of its people.

A structured adoption process helps a business avoid three common problems: paying for tools that do not solve an important problem, exposing sensitive information, and automating a weak process before it has been properly understood.
